A COMPREHENSIVE MODEL FOR THE EARLY DETECTION OF TYPE 2 DIABETES MELLITUS

SLORISK - THE SLOVENIAN DIABETES RISK QUESTIONNAIRE

Type 2 Diabetes is a long term disease, caused by a lack of insulin as a result of the pancreas failure or lower effect of insulin on the body tissues, muscles and the liver. Symptoms of pre-diabetes and Type 2 Diabetes are often invisible, so it is essential to perform prevention of disease and early detection. Untreated diabetes can lead to heart disease, stroke, high blood pressure, circulation problems, and damage of kidneys and eyes. Especially important activities for reducing the possibility of development the Type 2 Diabetestype 2 diabetes.

The following questions will help you to find out your risk of developing Type 2 Diabetes. You will be asked about risk factors for type 2 diabetes, such as gender, age, family history of diabetes, physical activity, blood pressure, eating habits and the history of high blood sugar. You will also be asked to measure your waist circumference.

Please answer the questions honestly and completely. Your answers are confidential and anonymous. The results of completed questionnaire may help you at understanding risk factors for Type 2 Diabetes.

Although the questionnaire is primarily aimed for adults between 40 and 74 years old, it can be applied to younger persons, but they must take into account the results with some caution.

Do you usually do some physical activity, such as brisk walking, for at least 30 minutes each day?
(The activity can be performed in the office or at home)

Yes No
Risk of developing Type 2 Diabetes increases by 86.8% in individuals who are not physically active. Regular physical activity is an essential element for regulating proper weight and to reduce risk factors for Type 2 Diabetes. World Health Organization recommends at least 30 minutes of physical activity per day or 150 minutes per week.

Have you ever been found to have high blood sugar?
(eg. in the case of periodic review, during an illness, during pregnancy?)

Yes No
The probability of development Type 2 Diabetes is higher by 64.4% in those individuals who have prior elevated blood sugar levels. History of high blood sugar can be caused by metabolic imbalance or pre-diabetes. An unusual increase in blood sugar level can be a warning sign for possible development of Type 2 Diabetes. Women who have had high blood sugar during pregnancy are at higher risk of developing Type 2 Diabetes.

The probability of developing Type 2 Diabetes increases by 77.1% in those individuals where someone within the wider family (grandparents, aunts, uncles, cousins, cousin) have type 2 diabetes. Likelihood for to developing Type 2 Diabetes is 68.6% higher in those individuals where someone in the immediate family (parents, brothers, sisters, and children) have Type 2 Diabetes. Type 2 Diabetes is often caused by genetic predisposition. It also may occur as a result to cultural factors and unhealthy lifestyles in the family.
